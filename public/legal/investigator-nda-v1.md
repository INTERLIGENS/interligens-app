# INTERLIGENS — Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement

**Version:** v1.0
**Effective date:** upon acceptance by the Investigator.

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between
**INTERLIGENS** ("INTERLIGENS") and the individual accepting it ("Investigator").

## 1. Purpose

The parties wish to exchange confidential information for the purpose of
collaborative investigation of on-chain fraud, promotional schemes, and
sanctioned entities ("Purpose").

## 2. Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by one
party to the other, whether orally, in writing, or in any other form, that
is marked as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be
confidential, including but not limited to:

- Investigation findings, draft case files, wallet attributions, and entity
  linkages.
- Sources, methods, and identities of informants.
- Unpublished intelligence, internal tooling, risk models, and scoring logic.
- Platform credentials, private keys, and access tokens.

## 3. Obligations

Each party shall:

1. Use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose.
2. Protect it with at least the same degree of care it uses for its own
   confidential information of similar sensitivity, and in no event less than
   reasonable care.
3. Not disclose it to any third party without the other party's prior
   written consent.
4. Limit internal access to personnel with a legitimate need to know.

## 4. Exclusions

The obligations in Section 3 do not apply to information that:

- Is or becomes publicly available without breach of this Agreement.
- Was already in the receiving party's possession, free of any obligation of
  confidence, prior to disclosure.
- Is rightfully received from a third party without obligation of confidence.
- Is independently developed without use of the disclosing party's
  Confidential Information.

## 5. Workspace & encryption

The Investigator acknowledges that the INTERLIGENS Case Workspace uses
client-side encryption. Titles, filenames, tags, notes, and raw file
contents are encrypted on the Investigator's device before transmission.
INTERLIGENS cannot read, recover, or reset this data. Loss of the
passphrase results in permanent, irrecoverable loss of the workspace
contents.

## 6. Term

This Agreement remains in effect for a period of three (3) years from the
date of acceptance, or until terminated in writing by either party.
Confidentiality obligations survive termination.

## 7. No license

Nothing in this Agreement grants any right or license, express or implied,
to any intellectual property of either party, except the limited right to
use the Confidential Information for the Purpose.

## 8. Governing law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of France. Any dispute arising under
this Agreement shall be submitted to the exclusive jurisdiction of the
competent courts of Paris.

## 9. Acceptance

By checking the acceptance box and submitting the onboarding form, the
Investigator acknowledges that they have read, understood, and agreed to
this Agreement in its entirety.
