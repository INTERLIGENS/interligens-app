INTERLIGENS — Non-Disclosure Agreement
Version: 1.0 | Language: EN

This Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between INTERLIGENS,
a Delaware corporation ("INTERLIGENS"), and the individual identified at signature
("Signatory"), in connection with the INTERLIGENS Trusted Investigator Program
("Program").

INTERLIGENS may provide the Signatory with free beta access to a private
investigator workspace and related tools in connection with the Program. Such
access is granted solely within the scope of the Program and does not create any
employment relationship, service obligation, or right to compensation unless
expressly agreed in a separate written agreement.

1. CONFIDENTIALITY
The Signatory agrees to hold in strict confidence all Confidential Information
received in connection with the Program. "Confidential Information" includes but
is not limited to: INTERLIGENS's investigative methods, analytical workflows,
entity mapping logic, scoring systems, case structures, internal labels,
correlations, compiled datasets, proprietary intelligence, internal documents,
materials, and any non-public information shared within the Program.

INTERLIGENS does not claim ownership of publicly available blockchain facts such
as public wallet addresses, public on-chain transactions, or public URLs. What
INTERLIGENS protects is the derived, analytical, methodological and structured
layer built on top of public data: methods, workflows, mappings, labels,
correlations, scores, case structures, exports, internal materials, compiled
datasets, and proprietary intelligence systems.

2. NON-DISCLOSURE
The Signatory shall not disclose, share, publish, communicate or otherwise make
available any Confidential Information to any third party without prior written
consent from INTERLIGENS.

3. HORIZONTAL COMPARTMENTALIZATION
The Signatory shall not seek to identify, disclose, or expose the identity,
cases, data, or activities of any other investigator participating in the Program,
except with the explicit authorization of INTERLIGENS.

4. NON-AUTHORIZED USE
The Signatory shall not use INTERLIGENS's Confidential Information, methods,
materials, outputs, processes, or proprietary intelligence for any purpose outside
the Program without prior written consent from INTERLIGENS.

5. NON-CIRCUMVENTION
The Signatory shall not use their participation in the Program to extract
confidential value, replicate INTERLIGENS's proprietary systems, or circumvent
the Program's rules and purpose.

6. SCOPE LIMITATION
This Agreement does not apply to information that is or becomes publicly known
through no breach by the Signatory, or that the Signatory can demonstrate was
independently developed without use of Confidential Information.

7. DURATION
The obligations of confidentiality set out in this Agreement shall remain in
force for a period of three (3) years from the date of signature, unless a longer
period is required by applicable law or separately agreed in writing.

8. GOVERNING LAW AND JURISDICTION
This Agreement, and any dispute, claim, or non-contractual obligation arising out
of or in connection with it, shall be governed by and construed in accordance
with the laws of the State of Delaware, United States, without regard to its
conflict-of-laws principles.

The state and federal courts located in Wilmington, Delaware shall have exclusive
jurisdiction over any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement, and
each Party irrevocably submits to the jurisdiction of such courts and waives any
objection based on venue or forum non conveniens.

Notwithstanding the foregoing, INTERLIGENS may seek temporary, preliminary,
conservatory, or injunctive relief in any court of competent jurisdiction to
prevent or limit any actual or threatened breach of confidentiality, misuse of
confidential information, or unauthorized disclosure of protected materials.

For signatories habitually resident in the European Union, this clause shall
apply to the fullest extent permitted by Regulation (EC) No 593/2008 and any
other mandatory applicable law. Nothing in this Agreement is intended to deprive
such signatories of any mandatory protection that cannot be waived by contract
under the law applicable to them.

The Parties acknowledge that this Agreement is entered into in a professional,
business-to-business or independent-contributor context and is not intended to
create a consumer or employment relationship.

9. LANGUAGE
This Agreement is available in English and French. The signatory selects the
language version displayed at signature. The accepted version is identified by
its ndaVersion, ndaLanguage, and ndaDocHash. In the event of discrepancy between
language versions, the English version shall prevail, except to the extent
mandatory law applicable to the signatory requires otherwise.
